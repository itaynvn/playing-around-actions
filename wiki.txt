<p><b>Vicious Cycle Software</b> was an American <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">video game development</a> company based in <a href="/wiki/Morrisville,_North_Carolina" title="Morrisville, North Carolina">Morrisville, North Carolina</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vicious_Cycle_Software&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vicious Cycle was founded in 2000 by Eric Peterson, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Ellis_(game_designer)" title="Dave Ellis (game designer)">Dave Ellis</a>, Marc Racine and Wayne Harvey after layoffs at the local <a href="/wiki/MicroProse" title="MicroProse">MicroProse</a> development studio (then a <a href="/wiki/Hasbro_Interactive" title="Hasbro Interactive">Hasbro Interactive</a> studio) forced several game developers into finding other work.  Racine resigned as Vice President and Director of Production in the Spring of 2005 to pursue other ventures. Ellis left the company in the Summer of 2000, but returned in 2005 to take a position as <a href="/wiki/Game_designer" class="mw-redirect" title="Game designer">game designer</a>. Vicious Cycle has released titles for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a>, <a href="/wiki/GameCube" title="GameCube">GameCube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> systems as well as the <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a> platform.
</p><p>In 2005, Vicious Cycle announced the opening of their <a href="/wiki/Monkey_Bar_Games" title="Monkey Bar Games">Monkey Bar Games</a> division.  Monkey Bar Games was focused on providing mass-market games to gamers of all ages. Monkey Bar Games released video games incorporating licensed characters from <a href="/wiki/Ben_10" title="Ben 10">Ben 10</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dora_the_Explorer" title="Dora the Explorer">Dora the Explorer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Curious_George" title="Curious George">Curious George</a>. A game was also released in late 2006 to coincide with the release of the <i><a href="/wiki/Flushed_Away" title="Flushed Away">Flushed Away</a></i> animated feature film.
</p><p>Also in 2005, Vicious Cycle announced the release of their <a href="/wiki/Vicious_Engine" title="Vicious Engine">Vicious Engine</a> <a href="/wiki/Game_engine" title="Game engine">game engine</a>. The Vicious Engine is a complete game development <a href="/wiki/Middleware" title="Middleware">middleware</a> solution for the PlayStation 2, PlayStation 3, Xbox, Xbox 360, PlayStation Portable, GameCube, Wii and Microsoft Windows. The Vicious Engine is one of the first game engines to offer full support for the PSP and Wii platforms.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On June 20, 2007, Vicious Cycle was acquired by <a href="/wiki/D3_Publisher" title="D3 Publisher">D3 Publisher</a>, making Vicious Cycle Software a subsidiary of D3Publisher of America and a second‐tier subsidiary of D3 Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Vicious Cycle released <i><a href="/wiki/Eat_Lead:_The_Return_of_Matt_Hazard" title="Eat Lead: The Return of Matt Hazard">Eat Lead: The Return of Matt Hazard</a></i> for Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3 on February 26, 2009. On October 1, 2009, they announced <i><a href="/wiki/Matt_Hazard:_Blood_Bath_and_Beyond" title="Matt Hazard: Blood Bath and Beyond">Matt Hazard: Blood Bath and Beyond</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Xbox_LIVE_Arcade" class="mw-redirect" title="Xbox LIVE Arcade">Xbox LIVE Arcade</a> and the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Store" title="PlayStation Store">PlayStation Store</a> for winter 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second version of the <a href="/wiki/Vicious_Engine" title="Vicious Engine">Vicious Engine</a>, V<sup>e2</sup>, was released on March 25, 2009, at the Game Developers Conference. It specifically features improvements for the Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Spring of 2014, Vicious Cycle was acquired by Little Orbit.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the final two games (<i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time:_Finn_%26_Jake_Investigations" title="Adventure Time: Finn &amp; Jake Investigations">Adventure Time: Finn &amp; Jake Investigations</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Fu_Panda:_Showdown_of_Legendary_Legends" title="Kung Fu Panda: Showdown of Legendary Legends">Kung Fu Panda: Showdown of Legendary Legends</a></i>) neared completion, several rounds of layoffs reduced the studio to a skeleton crew. Vicious Cycle closed its doors permanently in January 2016.
</p>
