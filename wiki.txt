<p>The <b>Dômes de Miage</b> are a line of mountain peaks in the south of the <a href="/wiki/Mont_Blanc_massif" title="Mont Blanc massif">Mont Blanc massif</a> that reach a height of 3,673 metres. The snow-covered <a href="/wiki/Ar%C3%AAte" title="Arête">arête</a> from which they rise is over three kilometres long. The six peaks in the chain are (from southwest to northeast)&#160;: L'Aiguille de la Bérangère (3,425 m), and unnamed tops known only from their heights as Dôme 3670, Dôme 3666, Dôme 3633, Dôme 3673 and Dôme 3672.
</p><p>The ridge of the dômes de Miage is considered one of the most striking in the Mont Blanc massif.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Normal_route" title="Normal route">normal route</a> starts at the <i>Refuge des Conscrits</i>, crosses the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%C3%A9-la-T%C3%AAte_glacier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tré-la-Tête glacier (page does not exist)">Tré-la-Tête glacier</a>, climbs to <i>Col des Dômes</i> (between tops 3633 and 3673) and follows the arête as far as La Bérangère (PD). A crossing from end to end runs from the <i>Refuge des Conscrits</i> to <a href="/wiki/Refuge_Durier" class="mw-redirect" title="Refuge Durier">refuge Durier</a> and is a little more difficult (AD). It is usually combined with the long traverse over the  <a href="/wiki/Aiguille_de_Bionnassay" title="Aiguille de Bionnassay">Aiguille de Bionnassay</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mont_Blanc" title="Mont Blanc">Mont Blanc</a>.
</p>
